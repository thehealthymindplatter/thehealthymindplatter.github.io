Background

The healthy mind platter model was created by Dr. Dan J. Siegel, Executive Director of the Mindsight Institute and clinical professor of psychiatry at the UCLA School of Medicine, and Dr. David Rock, Executive Director of the Neuroleadership Institute.

The mental health platter consists of seven mental activities that should be included in every day in order to optimise both focus and rest time for a healthy and happy mind.

Brain tools (for teens)

The inspiration for this site comes from a lecture and book written by Malin Gutestam. She is a teacher, educator, public speaker, coach and author. She is passionate about providing science-based tools that optimize brain health and learning for teens and young adults.

Malin Gutestam's book Brain tools for teens is structured around the mental health platter and is a practical actionable guide on how to improve mental wellbeing and learning. All the information and statements on this website comes from this book.

We believe that the insights and exercises provided by this book are extremely helpful for people in all stages of life.

We strongly recommend you to read this book for an all-encompassing perspective on your brain, mental wellbeing and learning. And we hope that you find this website useful for incorporating the learnings in your everyday life.
